The Importance of Medication Adherence

In the medical community, the phrase "medication adherence" simply means taking your medications exactly as prescribed by your doctor. While it sounds straightforward, statistics show that nearly 50% of patients do not take their medications as prescribed.

For chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, or asthma, skipping doses can drastically reduce the effectiveness of the treatment and lead to severe health complications. Here at MedBe Pharmacy, we see the real-world impact of both excellent adherence and poor adherence every single day.

Why Patients Skip Medications

There are many completely understandable reasons why people stop taking their medicines:

  • Forgetfulness: Life gets busy, and it's easy to miss a mid-day pill.
  • Cost: Medical expenses can stack up, causing some to ration their supply.
  • Feeling better: Often, patients feel completely cured and stop taking their antibiotics or blood pressure pills prematurely.
  • Side effects: Mild discomfort can deter patients from continuing a long-term prescription.
"Drugs don't work in patients who don't take them." — C. Everett Koop, Former U.S. Surgeon General

Strategies for Better Adherence

If you or a loved one struggles to remember to take daily medications, try implementing one of these simple strategies:

1. Use Pill Organizers

A weekly pill box with separated compartments for days and times takes the guesswork out of your routine entirely. You only have to organize your pills once a week!

2. Set Digital Reminders

Your smartphone is one of the best medical tools you have. Set a recurring daily alarm, or use dedicated health apps to track your medication intake.

3. Tie it to a Daily Habit

Take your medicine at the exact same time you do another non-negotiable daily task. For example, right after brushing your teeth in the morning, or right before your morning cup of coffee.
